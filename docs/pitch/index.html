The problem 02

A track record is a screenshot.

Tipster records are post-hoc, curated, unfalsifiable. Buyers can't tell edge from survivorship — and sellers with real edge can't prove it either. There is no neutral timestamp authority for a prediction.

Post-hoc
Posted after the whistle, or quietly deleted when wrong.
Curated
Wins amplified, losses vanish. Survivorship sells.
Unverifiable
No block, no timestamp, no way to check the claim.

The World Cup knockout window is peak scam season. A bettor with a real 15–9 record gets called a fraud on every win.

The solution 03

Payment and proof are the same act.

A pay-per-pick API on Injective EVM. You pay 0.05 USDC via x402 for the next knockout edge — model probability vs market odds, plus a conviction staking ladder. The x402 receipt (650 ms finality, immutable) timestamps the pick before kickoff.

After the match, a CLV engine scores every pick against the closing line and publishes it to a free public ledger. Buy the next pick for five cents; audit every previous pick for free.

The mechanic · the depth 04

Closing-line value is the luck-proof metric.

receipt-as-timestamp
The payment IS the notary
Block time < kickoff is checkable by anyone on the explorer. No custom contract needed — sha256(pick) is bound to the receipt in the same ledger row.
clv = implied(entry) − implied(close)
Skill, not variance
Positive CLV across a sample = real edge, independent of win/loss noise — the metric pro syndicates actually use, computed by code, not claimed in a tweet.
invariant receipts.count === ledger.rows — a missing loss is detectable by any auditor holding a receipt
